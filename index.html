HOIGS: Human–Object Interaction Gaussian Splatting from Monocular Videos

ICLR 2026 · Under Review

Paper (PDF) Video Results Project Materials

HOIGS reconstructs consistent humans, objects, and scenes from challenging monocular videos.

Abstract

Reconstructing dynamic scenes with complex human–object interactions is challenging for monocular videos. Existing Gaussian Splatting approaches either depend on human pose priors that miss dynamic objects or compress all motions into a single field, leading to blurry geometry and unstable contacts. We introduce Human–Object Interaction Gaussian Splatting (HOIGS), which explicitly models interaction-induced deformation between humans and objects through a cross-attention HOI module. HexPlane-based human deformation and Cubic Hermite Spline object trajectories provide complementary motion cues, enabling accurate contacts, occlusions, and manipulation. Experiments on HOSNeRF, BEHAVE, and in-the-wild videos show consistent improvements over human-centric and 4D Gaussian baselines.

Highlights

Interaction-Aware Modeling

Cross-attention HOI module captures bidirectional human–object motion cues for stable contact reasoning.
