Abstract

Reconstructing dynamic scenes with intricate human–object contact from a single video stream is challenging for Gaussian splatting frameworks that treat all motion uniformly. Human–Object Interaction Gaussian Splatting (HOIGS) explicitly disentangles human and object motion using hexplane-based human deformation and Cubic Hermite Splines for objects, then fuses them through a cross-attention HOI module. This interaction-aware composition improves deformation estimation under occlusion, manipulation, and contact, enabling high-fidelity reconstructions of human–object interactions from monocular inputs.
