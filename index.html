Abstract

Reconstructing dynamic scenes with complex human–object interactions is a fundamental challenge in computer vision and graphics. Existing Gaussian splatting methods either rely on human pose priors while neglecting dynamic objects or approximate all motions within a single field, limiting their ability to capture interaction-rich dynamics. HOIGS explicitly models interaction-induced deformation between humans and objects through a cross-attention based HOI module. Distinct deformation baselines extract complementary motion features—HexPlane for humans and Cubic Hermite Splines (CHS) for objects—allowing HOIGS to capture interdependent motions and improve deformation estimation in scenarios involving occlusion, contact, and manipulation. Comprehensive experiments demonstrate consistent improvements over state-of-the-art human-centric and 4D Gaussian approaches.

Framework

Human–Object Interaction Gaussian Splatting

HOIGS reconstructs scenes by independently modeling human and object deformations and injecting interaction-aware corrections through a mutual-attention HOI module.

  • Object deformation (CHS): Cubic Hermite Splines embed velocity vectors of keyframe Gaussians to prevent “melting” artifacts on texture-poor objects.
  • Human deformation (HexPlane): Time-varying HexPlane parameters provide fine-grained spatial–temporal detail for articulated humans.
  • Interaction reasoning: Cross-attention links human and object motion cues, refining SMPL-X estimates and object trajectories for physically plausible contact.
